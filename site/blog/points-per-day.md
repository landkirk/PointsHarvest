## The Short Answer

After the 2026 overhaul, the most you can earn per day from Bing searching is **15 points at Member, 30 at Silver, and 60 at Gold** — a single cap shared across PC and mobile. Add the Daily Set (15 points) and the streak bonus (~15/day), and a realistic daily total is roughly:

| Tier | Daily searches | Daily Set | Streak | **Typical daily total** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Member | ~15 | 15 | ~15 | **~45** |
| Silver | ~30 | 15 | ~15 | **~60** |
| Gold | ~60 | 15 | ~15 | **~90** |

Gold users also get a 420-point monthly tier bonus and are eligible for the STAR Bonus, which pushes a realistic Gold day closer to **~110 points** once those are spread across the month.

If you've read a guide claiming 250 or 500 points a day, it was describing the retired Level 1 / Level 2 system. Those numbers stopped being true when [the new Member/Silver/Gold dashboard reached the US in mid-July 2026](/blog/rewards-2026-overhaul/).

> **Note:** Microsoft has been adjusting values region by region since January, and the US migration is very recent. The figures here are corroborated across multiple migrated regions, but **your own dashboard is the source of truth.** Check it before planning around any number on this page.

## What Counts Toward Your Daily Total

Four things make up the bulk of a normal day.

**Bing searches — the tier cap.** This is the number most people mean when they ask how many points they can earn per day, and it's the number the overhaul cut hardest. Under Level 2 a US user could pull 150 points/day from PC search *plus* another 100 from mobile. Now it's a shared ceiling of ~15 / ~30 / ~60 depending on tier, and **searching on your phone no longer opens a second bucket.**

**The Daily Set — 15 points.** Three rotating tiles at 5 points each. Microsoft cut these from 10 points per tile in March 2026, which halved the set from 30 points/day to 15. Tiles rotate between quizzes, polls, and simple article click-throughs.

**The streak bonus — roughly 15 points/day in effect.** It actually pays about 210 points across 14 days rather than crediting daily, but averaged out that's ~15/day. It's available across tiers and it rewards showing up rather than searching hard.

**"Keep earning" and Explore on Bing tiles — variable.** A handful of points, some days more than others. The count and value change week to week, so there's no honest fixed number to quote here.

Two categories that used to matter and no longer do the same work: mobile searching (folded into the shared cap) and several older activities — Edge browsing, Outlook reading, the puzzle mosaic — whose status under the new US dashboard isn't clearly confirmed yet.

## How Long Does It Take to Reach 13,000 Points?

This one comes up constantly, and it's usually a Roblox question. **13,000 points is the Roblox digital card tier** in the US catalog (it's also what the Plasma Wings exclusive costs), which is why so many people target that specific number rather than a round one.

Worth knowing before you start: **if you want a plain gift card, you need fewer points than that.** A $10 Xbox or Microsoft gift card runs about 10,000–10,500 points in the US, so a card is roughly three weeks closer than the Roblox tier. Prices move by region and by card, Gold's up-to-10% redemption discount lowers them, and Microsoft periodically runs Rewards sales — check your own redeem page before committing to a target.

Here's the timeline at each tier, assuming you do the searches and the Daily Set every day:

| Tier | Daily total | Days to 13,000 | Roughly |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Member | ~45 | ~290 | ~9–10 months |
| Silver | ~60 | ~215 | ~7 months |
| Gold | ~90 | ~145 | ~5 months |
| Gold + tier bonus + typical STAR | ~110 | ~115 | ~4 months |
| Gold + full Xbox/Game Pass grind | ~400 | ~33 | ~1 month |

The honest read: **for a non-gamer at Gold, a Roblox card is a four-to-five month project** — and a $10 gift card, at ~10,000 points, is about three to four. Under the old system the same routine got you to either one in roughly six weeks. The Xbox and Game Pass side is now the majority of the program's value for anyone who has Game Pass Ultimate — our [full points breakdown](/blog/maximum-rewards-points/) covers those categories in detail.

Skipping days costs more than it used to, because a broken streak takes both the streak bonus and your STAR consistency with it.

## What About Per Month?

Multiply through and a Gold-tier user lands at roughly **3,400 points/month** from the reliably countable categories: ~1,800 from searches, ~450 from the Daily Set, ~450 from streaks, 420 from the Gold tier bonus, and ~300 from a typical STAR Bonus payout.

Silver comes in near **1,800/month**, and Member near **1,350/month**.

At the rough guide of 1,000 points ≈ $1 in Microsoft credit, Gold is about **$3.40/month**. ## Why Most People Earn Less Than the Maximum

The caps above are ceilings, and three things regularly keep people under them.

**Search throttling.** Firing off searches back to back trips a cooldown, and searches during that window earn nothing. If your counter freezes well short of your cap, this is usually why. Spacing searches out fixes it; hammering the search box harder does not.

**Broken streaks.** Missing a day resets the streak bonus, and since it pays across 14-day blocks, one missed day can cost more than a full day's searching.

**The STAR Bonus is not a reliable number.** It advertises up to 2,100 points/month, but by Microsoft's own figures **roughly 80% of users receive under 300**. It's awarded by an opaque algorithm that judges whether your search behavior looks consistent and natural across the month, with no progress indicator and no explanation of the result. Plan around ~300 and treat anything above it as a surprise. We covered how it works — and what it means if you automate — in [the overhaul explainer](/blog/rewards-2026-overhaul/).

## Quick Answers

**How many Microsoft Rewards points can you earn a day?** Roughly 45 at Member, 60 at Silver, and 90 at Gold, counting searches, the Daily Set, and the streak bonus. Gold users with the tier bonus and a typical STAR payout average closer to 110.

**How many Bing search points can I get per day?** 15 at Member, 30 at Silver, 60 at Gold — shared across PC and mobile.

**How many Microsoft Rewards points per month?** About 1,350 at Member, 1,800 at Silver, and 3,400 at Gold from Bing-side activities. Xbox and Game Pass can add ~8,500 more if you have Game Pass Ultimate.

**How long does it take to get 13,000 Microsoft points?** About four to five months at Gold doing the daily routine, seven months at Silver, and nine to ten at Member. Roughly one month if you're also doing the full Xbox and Game Pass grind. 13,000 is the Roblox card tier — a $10 gift card needs only ~10,000, so it arrives a few weeks sooner.

**Does searching on my phone add extra points?** No. PC and mobile share a single daily cap under the new system.

**Why did my daily limit drop to 15?** You're on the Member tier. Gold requires around 750 lifetime points, which is a low bar most long-time users have already cleared — and tier status is permanent now, so you only have to reach it once.
