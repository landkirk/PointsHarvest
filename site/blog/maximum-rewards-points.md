## The Short Answer

Microsoft rebuilt the Rewards program in 2026, and the new Member/Silver/Gold system [reached the United States in mid-July](/blog/rewards-2026-overhaul/). Every number on this page has been rewritten around it. If you read an older version of this post — or any guide still quoting 23,000 points a month — those figures described the retired Level 1 / Level 2 system and no longer apply.

Under the new system, a **Gold-tier user in the United States** can expect roughly:

- **~3,100 points/month** from reliable, countable Bing activities
- **plus 0–2,100 points/month** from the new STAR Bonus, though ~80% of users receive under 300
- **plus ~8,500 points/month** from Xbox and Game Pass, if you have Game Pass Ultimate and game daily

So a realistic ceiling for a non-gamer is around **3,400 points/month** (~$3.40 in Microsoft credit), and a dedicated gamer maxing out everything lands somewhere near **12,000 points/month** (~$12). Both are well down from what the same effort produced in early 2026.

**Points Harvest automates roughly 2,700 of those points per month** — the search farming, Daily Set tiles, Explore on Bing cards, and More Activities tiles — in about 2–5 minutes a day. Running daily also builds the consistency the STAR Bonus rewards, so **~3,000/month is the more realistic figure** once a typical STAR payout is included.

> **Note on freshness:** the US migrated to the new dashboard around **July 16, 2026**, and Microsoft has been adjusting values region by region since January. The tier caps and Daily Set values below are corroborated across multiple migrated regions. Several older earning categories — Edge browsing, Outlook reading, the puzzle mosaic — have not been clearly confirmed under the new US dashboard, and are flagged as unconfirmed rather than given invented numbers. **Your dashboard is the source of truth.**

> **Account restrictions:** Microsoft applies restrictions that can reduce your actual earnings. Newer accounts may see lower caps, more frequent CAPTCHAs, or delayed crediting. Microsoft also suspends accounts for automation, multiple accounts, VPN use, and cross-region redemption. Your mileage will vary.

## The Full Breakdown

Points Harvest reads these cards, extracts the search queries, and completes them, retrying with an alternative query if one doesn't register. The number and value of cards varies week to week, so we're not going to quote you a monthly total we can't stand behind.

#### Phase 2: Daily Set (~900/month)

- **Daily Set tiles** — 15 points/day = **450/month**
  Three rotating tiles that refresh daily. **Microsoft cut these from 10 points per tile to 5 in March 2026**, halving the set from 30 points/day to 15. Tiles can be quizzes, polls, or article click-throughs. Points Harvest completes the simple ones automatically and pauses on quizzes and polls so you can answer them, then resumes.

  *Effort: ~1 minute/day for interactive tiles.*

- **Streak bonus** — ~210 points per 14 days = **~450/month**
  The new system rewards consistency rather than volume. Showing up every day builds the streak; missing days breaks it. Points Harvest earns this as a byproduct of running daily.

  *Effort: zero — earned automatically by running the extension daily.*

#### Phase 3: More Activities (variable, small)

- **"Keep earning" tiles** — a handful of points per week
  Additional tiles below the daily set, each linking to a Bing search page. Points Harvest opens the tile, dwells, closes it, and validates completion. Tiles requiring real interaction (puzzles, quizzes, install prompts) are skipped automatically. The contribution is small but effortless.

  *Effort: zero — fully automated.*

- **Daily searches** — up to 60 points/day at Gold = **~1,800/month**
  This is where the overhaul bites hardest. Daily search points are now capped by tier at roughly **15 (Member) / 30 (Silver) / 60 (Gold)** points per day, and **PC and mobile share a single cap** — searching on your phone no longer opens a second bucket. Under the old Level 2 system this category alone was worth 150 points/day from PC plus another 100 from mobile.

  Points Harvest reads your cap directly from the Rewards dashboard API rather than assuming a fixed number, so it stops at whatever your tier actually allows.

  *Effort: zero — fully automated.*

> **Subtotal: roughly 2,700 points/month automated at Gold tier**, depending on how many activity cards your dashboard serves up — or **~3,000 including a typical STAR Bonus**, which running daily helps earn. A full run takes about 2–5 minutes.

### The STAR Bonus: 0–2,100 Points

The **Bing STAR Bonus** is new, and it deserves its own section because it behaves unlike anything else in the program.

It's a monthly allocation of up to 2,100 points, credited at month's end, awarded by an algorithm that judges whether your search behavior looks consistent, authentic, and natural. There's no task list, no progress indicator, and no explanation of the result.

Two numbers matter here. First, per Microsoft's own figures, **around 80% of users receive under 300 points** — the 2,100 ceiling is largely theoretical. Second, **patterned or bot-like search behavior resets STAR progress**, by Microsoft's explicit design.

> **If you automate, treat this as a risk rather than a write-off.** What the check targets is mechanical patterning — evenly-spaced, round-the-clock searching. Points Harvest draws its delays from a long-tailed human distribution instead of a fixed interval, and offers a Stealth speed setting that spreads a run out further, so a normal daily session isn't the obvious target. But the algorithm is unauditable, so nobody can promise it passes.

**What to actually pencil in:** the thing STAR rewards most clearly is turning up every day, which is exactly what a daily automated run does — so there's a reasonable case that automation *helps* here rather than hurting. We use **~300 points/month** in the totals on this page, which is the upper edge of what four out of five users receive. Treat it as a plausible middle estimate, not a floor and certainly not the 2,100 ceiling.

*Effort: zero, but the payout is unpredictable by design.*

### Manual Bing Activities: Status Unconfirmed

Several categories from the old system have not been clearly confirmed under the new US dashboard. They may persist, may have been devalued, or may be gone. Rather than repeat stale figures, here's what to check on your own dashboard:

- **Mobile search** — **folded into the shared daily cap.** This is confirmed: mobile no longer earns a separate 100 points/day. This alone removes ~3,000 points/month from what the old breakdown promised.
- **Read news to earn** — still appears for many users; per-article values were cut in the March devaluation.
- **Browse with Edge** — status unconfirmed under the new dashboard.
- **Read Outlook emails** — status unconfirmed.
- **Bing app check-in** — still present, but users report the daily value was reduced.
- **Puzzle piece mosaic** — status unconfirmed; it ran on a 3-week cycle and may not have survived the redesign.

If these all survived at reduced values, they'd add somewhere in the low hundreds of points per month. We'll update this section as the US dashboard settles.

### Xbox and Game Pass: ~8,545 Points

These require **Xbox Game Pass Ultimate** and regular gaming. We have no reporting indicating the Xbox side was touched by the tier overhaul, which was focused on Bing search earning — but treat these as the least-recently-verified numbers on the page.

- **Game Pass weekly streak (4x multiplier)** — 700/week = **3,010/month**. *Effort: ~10–15 min/week, but never miss a week.*
- **Game Pass weekly PC bonus** — 260/week = **1,118/month**. *Effort: ~15–30 min/week.*
- **Game Pass weekly console bonus** — 260/week = **1,118/month**. *Effort: ~15–30 min/week.*
- **Play a PC game** — 20/day = **600/month**. *Effort: ~5 min/day, or zero if you already game.*
- **Play a console game** — 20/day = **600/month**. *Effort: ~5 min/day.*
- **Play a Game Pass game** — 20/day = **600/month**. Stacks with the above. *Effort: ~5 min/day.*
- **Use the Xbox app** — 130/week = **559/month**. *Effort: ~1 min/day.*
- **Play Jewel** — 10/day = **300/month**. *Effort: ~2–3 min/day.*
- **Game Pass monthly 8-pack** — **560/month**. *Effort: ~2–4 hours across the month.*
- **Game Pass monthly 4-pack** — **80/month**. *Effort: ~1–2 hours across the month.*

Under the new system, **Xbox and Game Pass are now the majority of the program's total value** for anyone who has Game Pass Ultimate. That's a notable inversion: the Bing side used to be the bigger half.

## A Realistic Monthly Estimate

- **Points Harvest only (~3,000 pts, ~$3/month, ~$36/year)** — Run the extension daily, spend a minute on quizzes and polls. That's ~2,700 in countable daily earnings plus a typical ~300 STAR Bonus. Best effort-to-reward ratio, though the reward is smaller than it was.

- **Points Harvest + easy manual tasks (~3,700–4,300 pts, ~$3.70–4.30/month)** — Add news reading and app check-ins. The shared search cap means adding phone searches no longer helps.

- **Full grind including Xbox (~12,000 pts, ~$12/month, ~$144/year)** — Everything above, plus Game Pass Ultimate and daily gaming on PC and console. Realistic only if you're already an active gamer.

> **On redemption values:** the rough guide has long been ~1,000 points ≈ $1 in Microsoft credit, but users reported redemption costs *increasing* alongside the July US migration. Gold tier's up-to-10% redemption discount offsets some of that. Check current costs before assuming a dollar value.

## Is It Still Worth It?

For a few minutes a day, ~$36/year in Microsoft credit is a smaller prize than it was in April, and it's fair to conclude the grind isn't worth it anymore — plenty of long-time users on r/MicrosoftRewards have reached exactly that conclusion since the overhaul.

What hasn't changed is the effort-to-reward ratio at the automated end. If the daily routine costs you two minutes, a reduced payout is still close to free. If it costs you an hour, the new numbers probably don't justify it.
